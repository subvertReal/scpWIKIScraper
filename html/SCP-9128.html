SCP-9128
rating: +126

Item #: SCP-9128

Object Class: Safe

Special Containment Procedures: When not in use, SCP-9128 is to be stored in a sealed container in a medium-threat-level item locker. SCP-9128's containment chamber is to be fitted with a high-power fire suppression system.

Description: SCP-9128 is a book titled the book of all things by dado. Despite its size, SCP-9128 has an infinite number of pages. SCP-9128 is a dictionary containing all possible combinations of letters and their definitions.

Transcript of SCP-9128 :

a
the first letter of the alphabet

aa
abbreviation: alcoholics anonymous, a support group for alcoholics

aaa
small battery, often used in small electronic devices

aaaa
onomatopoeia: yelling

aaaaa
onomatopoeia: yelling

aaaaaa
onomatopoeia: yelling

aaaaaaa
astro-afro-antarctico-amer-asian auto association: a fictional roadside assistance service

aaaaaaaa
onomatopoeia: yelling

aaaaaaaaa
onomatopoeia: yelling for a long time

[multiple entries skipped for redundancy]

a(25)1 onomatopoeia: yelling for an extremely long time onomatopoeia: yelling for a very long time

[multiple entries skipped for redundancy]

a(3192)2 (Page 30743) onomatopoeia: yelling for an extremely long time
This is the final definition where all text fits on one page.


There seems to be no limit to the length of a word; therefore, it is impossible to get past the “a” section through conventional means. Content found during its discovery provides evidence that SCP-9128 contains all possible “words”. See addendum.

Studies into the noosphere have shown that all definitions inside SCP-9128 are correct.

TESTING LOG 1

DATE: 13, April, 2026

FORWARD: Testing to determine the nature of SCP-9128's pages

Test A: A cut was made into a page.
Result: Page displayed qualities similar to normal paper. The page did not heal itself.

Test B: Page #86 was removed from SCP-9128.
Result: SCP-9128's weight or density did not change.

Test C: Collect a core sample from SCP-9128.
Result: Despite being 7.3 cm thick, the drill was able to go approximately 1.2 meters into SCP-9128 before testing was stopped.

[END LOG]

It appears that SCP-9128 is infinitely dense. I am prohibiting invasive testing on the spine and cover of the book for fear of destabilizing it. -Dr. A.J.

TESTING LOG 2

DATE: 18, April, 2026,

FORWARD: Testing to determine how SCP-9128 defines words

Test A: SCP-9128 was brought into a total noospheric isolation chamber

Subject was told to define a(87)3 as: “The letter a repeated 87 times”
Result: Definition changed. Definition returned to previous when chamber was deactivated.

Test B: Subject was told all words longer than 10 letters don't exist.
Transcript of SCP-9128:

a
the first letter of the alphabet

[a(2)4-a(10)5 skipped for redundancy]

aaaaaaaaab
improper spelling of ab

Test C: A joke was added to the popular TV show ██████████████. The bit involved a character defining “yelling for a very long time” as: At least 25 “a”s long. The bit was heavily promoted across social media through foundation bots. 2 days after the episode aired, a(25) changed to: “onomatopoeia: yelling for a very long time”. Previously, a(25) was defined as: “onomatopoeia: yelling for an extremely long time”. “onomatopoeia: yelling for an extremely long time” is now defined at a(31)6 and onwards.

[END LOG]

This would be an extremely easy and cheap way to study the noosphere, too bad we can't actually use it. -Dr. A.J.

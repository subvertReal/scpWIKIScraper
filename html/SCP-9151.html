SCP Foundation

Secure, Contain, Protect

SCP-9151 (The Nuclear Pest)

Item #: SCP-9151
Object Class: Safe (Pending Euclid upgrade under O5 review)

Special Containment Procedures:
Each SCP-9151 instance is to be housed individually in standard Anomalous Fauna containment modules. All sites must be located at least 50 kilometers from any known or suspected sources of nuclear material, including reactors, research facilities, weapons stockpiles, or spent fuel repositories.
Containment habitat requirements:
Minimum volume: 1 m³ per instance

Integrated water feature: Natural or artificial spring, non-chlorinated

Indigenous flora: Per Flora Protocol 9151-Alpha

Controlled radiation station: Output must not exceed 10 millisieverts/hour

No SCP-9151 instance is to be exposed to uncontained nuclear material under any circumstance. Violation constitutes an immediate XK-Class scenario risk.
Continuous audiovisual monitoring is mandatory. Bimonthly radiation audits must be performed by Foundation-certified nuclear physicists. Civilian contact is to be intercepted with extreme prejudice; full amnestic sanitization to follow under Protocol DS-505-A3.

Description:
SCP-9151 refers to a bioluminescent caterpillar-like species discovered following nuclear testing in Bikini Atoll, 1958. Specimens are translucent, measuring ~7–10 cm in length. Internal anatomy is visibly exposed beneath their gelatinous dermal layers, including a pulsating red vascular system and twin luminescent yellow ocular nodes. When inactive, their movement is slow and deliberate, often accompanied by faint rhythmic chirping.
Genetic sequencing reveals substantial overlap with Manduca sexta (the tobacco hornworm), yet SCP-9151 displays unique mutagenic markers not attributable to known radiation damage. These mutations are localized around a dense, radiation-absorbing organ located in the abdomen, nicknamed the Rad Sac.
This organ actively metabolizes ionizing radiation, expanding by approximately 0.24mm per millisievert absorbed. Green luminescence intensifies in tandem with exposure levels. Once the Rad Sac exceeds a saturation threshold (approx. equivalent to five Hiroshima-level detonations), the specimen undergoes spontaneous critical rupture, releasing highly irradiated organic matter in all directions.
This detonation produces no thermal release. Instead, survivors describe an instantaneous pulse of green light followed by structural breakdown of matter within a 6–12 meter radius, including biological tissue at the cellular level. The mechanism is not fully understood.

Addendum 9151-A: Incident Logs
Incident A-1 — Site-62 Containment Breach (██/██/1974)
Involved: Dr. Marcus Rosen (Lead Parazoologist), Agent Isaac Keller (Security)
Medium: Security Audio Feed – Habitat Wing 3B
[02:39] Rosen: Oh god—it’s out! The door wasn’t sealed—how the hell did—
[02:46] [Audio records a sound resembling heavy, wet velvet sliding across a chalkboard—a sharp, dissonant "thrum" that vibrates the recording equipment itself.]
[02:49] Keller: Move! Now—
[02:53] [Audio feed cuts. Static for 18 seconds.]
Postmortem: Entire containment wing was vaporized in a 14-meter radius. No biological traces of Rosen or Keller recovered. Nearby monitoring equipment permanently irradiated. Investigation inconclusive.

Incident A-2 — Unauthorized Experiment (██/██/1998)
Subject: Dr. Elias Granger
Context: Off-protocol experimentation, Lab-14
Footage Recovered: Partial (Security Cam 14-B)
[23:21] (Granger enters lab, places SCP-9151 in glass case beside depleted uranium rod.)
[23:22] Specimen begins to glow faintly. Chirping audible.
[23:25] (Glow intensifies. Granger backs away, begins dictating into recorder.)
[23:27] Sudden flash. Green pulse. Static.
[23:29] (Feed clears. Granger stands motionless. His skin now translucent. Eyes still glowing.)
(He collapses. Over 40 seconds, body decays into phosphorescent particulate dust.)
Result: Lab-14 sealed indefinitely. Granger's notes confiscated. No disciplinary record filed—at request of O5 Command.

Addendum 9151-B: Interviews
Interview B-1 — Dr. Emily Zhao (Foundation Historian)
Following her investigation into Siberian oral traditions regarding the Tunguska Event (1908).
Agent Reynolds: Walk us through what you found in the texts.
Dr. Zhao: The villagers said the sky exploded. A fireball. Trees knocked flat. We’ve heard that before. But then… they talked about the worms.
Reynolds: Worms?
Zhao: "Crawling lights" emerging from the crater. Glowing green. People nearby sickened, skin went translucent, bones brittle. Radiation poisoning—decades before radiation was a concept.
Reynolds: Coincidence?
Zhao: It’s not isolated. Ancient Egypt. Han dynasty China. France during the Black Death. Different languages, same story. Translucent worms. Green light. Singing.
Reynolds: Singing?
Zhao: Yes. Rhythmic chirping. Described as "prayers" or "songs of judgment."
(Interview ends abruptly. Agent Reynolds noted as visibly shaken.)

Interview B-2 — Agent Phillip Chen (Marshall, Carter, and Dark Infiltration Debrief)
Dr. Hart: You said it wasn’t about weapons.
Chen: Not just weapons. MC&D tried merging SCP-9151 with another isotope. One from the anomalous vaults—SCP-███.
Hart: (Sound of shuffling papers) Right. And the result? Can we move this along?
Chen: It absorbed everything. Radiation, anomalous charge, mass. Then… it started coordinating.
Hart: (Sighs) Define “coordinating.”
Chen: The specimens synchronized. Moved as a group. Chirping in patterns. One researcher swore they were speaking.
Hart: Speaking how? People hear patterns in white noise all the time, Phillip.
Chen: He said they sang. One phrase over and over.
Hart: Which was…?
Chen: "They sing the song of creation."
(Pause)
Hart: I think we are done here. This is becoming anecdotal. (Sounds of scraping chair on floor as Dr. Hart moves to leave)
Chen: Transparent. Organ systems still glowing. Like Granger.
Hart: (Returns to chair and clicks pen) Tell me more.
[End log]
Follow up note - Dr. Hart (Post-Interview +3 Weeks): I found myself humming it this morning in the shower. I didn’t even realize I knew the melody.

Addendum 9151-C: Historical Documentation (Selected)
Ancient Egyptian Papyrus (c. 1300 BCE):
“Ra sent crawling lights to consume the unclean. They sang across the sand. Only bones and glass remained.”

Han Dynasty Scroll Fragment (202 BCE):
“From sky-fire came singing worms. Glowed like emeralds. Touched the careless. Left only dust.”

French Monastic Manuscript (1360 CE):
“By night the monks saw them: tiny glowing beasts that chanted beneath the moon. Villagers grew glassy-eyed, brittle. Soon, they vanished.”

Addendum 9151-D: Personal Logs
Dr. Marcus Rosen, Journal Entry (D-7):
"I’ve stopped sleeping. Their chirping doesn’t stop. I’m beginning to understand it. Not language. Not really. It’s music. Structured. Predictive. I think… it’s a kind of memory. Like they’re remembering something that hasn’t happened yet."

Agent Isaac Keller, Unsent Field Memo:
"Rosen keeps saying they're communicating. He might be right. It’s not just noise—there’s a rhythm. A message.
And last night I heard them too. I don’t know what it meant, but it scared the hell out of me.
We shouldn’t be feeding them. We shouldn’t be near them. They’re waiting."

Addendum 9151-E: Internal Memo (O5 Redacted)
To: All Containment Site Directors
From: O5-██
*Effective immediately, SCP-9151 is under active Euclid reclassification consideration.
Given accumulated evidence of communicative behavior, nuclear sensitivity, and repeated historic emergence, containment must be prioritized over curiosity.
This species may not be a mutation. It may be a recurrence.*
“Study nothing. Touch nothing. Feed nothing.”

Addendum 9151-F: Active Hypotheses (Level 4 Access Only)
Prehistoric Remnant: SCP-9151 may be a survivor of pre-human life forms adapted to cosmic radiation bursts.

Hive Intelligence: Potential for distributed cognition and intra-species coordination.

Extra-Dimensional Origin: Their resilience to known physics suggests partial existence in non-Euclidean space.

Response Entity: SCP-9151 may function as a planetary immune response to nuclear threat.

Catalyst Mechanism: If communicative, SCP-9151 may be preparing—not warning.

Final Note — Dr. Hart
"We've stared down chaos gods and machine intelligences, but this… This feels older.
Not malicious. Not malevolent. Just inevitable.
Like something we've woken up too many times.
Jesus Christ, I have a headache just thinking about it.
And one day, they might decide to sing louder.
We need to be ready for that."
